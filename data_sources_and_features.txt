PROJECT: Motor_Failures_Prediction
TITLE: Data Sources & Feature Engineering

--------------------------------------------------
OVERVIEW:
This dataset integrates condition monitoring variables from industrial equipment, combining vibration, thermal, electrical, and load-related data.

The objective is to detect early signs of motor failure and enable predictive maintenance strategies.

--------------------------------------------------
DATA SOURCES AND VARIABLE ORIGIN:

1. VIBRATION MONITORING SYSTEM
Variables:
- vib_rms_mms
- vib_peak_to_peak_mms

Taken from:
Vibration sensors mounted on motor housings and bearing locations.

Reason:
Vibration is one of the earliest indicators of mechanical issues such as imbalance, misalignment, or bearing wear.

--------------------------------------------------
2. THERMAL MONITORING SYSTEM
Variables:
- bearing_temp_c

Taken from:
Temperature sensors installed near bearings.

Reason:
Elevated temperature indicates friction, lubrication issues, or early degradation of components.

--------------------------------------------------
3. ELECTRICAL MONITORING SYSTEM
Variables:
- motor_current_a
- dominant_freq_hz

Taken from:
Motor current sensors and signal frequency analysis.

Reason:
Changes in electrical behavior can indicate load variation, internal faults, or abnormal operating conditions.

--------------------------------------------------
4. OPERATING CONDITIONS
Variables:
- load_pct

Taken from:
Machine load monitoring systems or production control signals.

Reason:
Higher loads increase stress on the motor and accelerate wear mechanisms.

--------------------------------------------------
5. TARGET VARIABLE (MAINTENANCE / FAILURE RECORDS)
Variables:
- incipient_failure

Taken from:
Maintenance logs and condition-based failure identification records.

Reason:
Indicates whether the motor is showing early-stage failure conditions. Used as the classification target.

--------------------------------------------------
VARIABLE-BY-VARIABLE SUMMARY:

vib_rms_mms
Taken from:
Vibration sensors
Why it was included:
Measures overall vibration energy, useful for detecting general mechanical degradation.

vib_peak_to_peak_mms
Taken from:
Vibration sensors
Why it was included:
Captures extreme vibration amplitudes, useful for detecting shocks or localized faults.

bearing_temp_c
Taken from:
Temperature sensors
Why it was included:
Detects overheating conditions associated with friction or lubrication failure.

motor_current_a
Taken from:
Electrical monitoring
Why it was included:
Reflects load behavior and electrical anomalies.

dominant_freq_hz
Taken from:
Frequency analysis of signals
Why it was included:
Identifies changes in vibration or electrical frequency patterns linked to faults.

load_pct
Taken from:
Machine operation system
Why it was included:
Represents stress level on the equipment.

incipient_failure
Taken from:
Maintenance and diagnostic records
Why it was included:
Defines early-stage failure condition for predictive modeling.

--------------------------------------------------
FEATURE ENGINEERING LOGIC:

The dataset combines mechanical, thermal, and electrical signals to provide a comprehensive view of equipment condition.

Each variable contributes to detecting different failure modes, improving model robustness.

--------------------------------------------------
BUSINESS LOGIC:

Motor failures are not random.

They are preceded by measurable changes in vibration, temperature, electrical behavior, and load conditions.

By capturing these signals, it is possible to detect failures before they occur.

--------------------------------------------------
GOAL:

Predict early failure conditions to:
- Reduce unplanned downtime
- Improve MTBF (Mean Time Between Failures)
- Optimize maintenance scheduling
- Reduce maintenance costs

--------------------------------------------------
NOTE:

Data originates from industrial condition monitoring systems, integrating sensor data and maintenance records from real equipment operation.
